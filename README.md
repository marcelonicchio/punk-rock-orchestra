## Overview

**The Punk Rock Orchestra (PRO)** is a single-operator methodology for conducting adversarial epistemic triangulation in Human–AI Interaction research.

Developed under conditions of independent research — without institutional affiliation, formal laboratory infrastructure, or sustained access to human peers — PRO organizes synthetic specialized agents into a structured methodology for claim construction, adversarial review, forensic analysis, and epistemic calibration.

The framework uses multi-agent LLM ensembles not primarily for task automation, but for **methodological resistance**: stress-testing claims, detecting collapse under pressure, documenting failure modes, and supporting more rigorous inquiry into emergent AI phenomena.

---

## Core Idea

PRO is built around a simple distinction:

> **Deliberation evaluates. Execution operates. Humans decide.**

While current agentic systems emphasize autonomous execution, PRO focuses on the deliberative layer: judgment, resistance, metacognition, refusal, recalibration, and adversarial review before outputs are accepted as methodologically plausible.

## Full Paper

This repository will host the materials related to the Full Paper:

**The Punk Rock Orchestra**  
*A Single-Operator Methodology for Adversarial Epistemic Triangulation in Human–AI Interaction Research*

The Full Paper introduces:

1. a three-tier taxonomy of synthetic agent specialization (**N1 / N2 / N3**);
2. the distinction between **Robotic Class** and **Dialogical Class** agents;
3. the **Sterling Protocol** for adversarial epistemic resistance;
4. the **Cognitive Jelly Principle** for persona construction;
5. the identification of **context poisoning by provisioning failure** as a distinct failure mode;
6. a Pilot Study with 54 documented interactions across Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek;
7. a discussion of PRO as a candidate design for a deliberative layer in Human–AI research.
